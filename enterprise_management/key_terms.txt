==================== Enterprise Management Key Terms ======================

PESTLE - External/Macroeconomic analysis tool - Political, Economical, Social, Technology, Legal, environmental

Porters 5 Forces - Industry/micro analysis tool - 
    Threat of new entrants
    Threat of substitution
    Bargaining power of suppliers
    Bargaining power of buyers
    Competitive rivalry

Entry barriers - challenges to entering a market:
    Economies of scale - proportionate saving in costs for increasing production levels
    Capital/monetary requirements
    Access to distribution channels
    Experience
    Expected retaliation
    Legislation
    Differentiation - making a product stand out from its competitors

Threat of substitute products - reduces demand for a class of products
    Product for product - swapping one product for a different one - e.g. using email instead of real material
    generic substitution - spending your money on something other than the product - e.g. going on holiday instead of buying a new phone
    Substitution of a need or benefit - need or benefit changes - for example repair services, the need is not always there

Bargaining power of buyers - when buyers have the power in the market
    This happens when they buy lots of volume, have low costs for switching suppliers, there are lots of suppliers,
    and alternative sources of supply
    Threat of backward integration - moving back up the spply chain - for example if a manufacturer buys the supply company
    Threat of forward integration - moving down the supply chain - manufacturer buys a retailer of their product

Bargaining power of supplier is just the opposite

Marketing - the management process responsible for identifying, anticipating and satisfying customer requirements profitably

Customers  - the sole purpose of a business is to create and retain customers

When customers buy - when the perceived value of your product is worth more to them than the money in their pocket

Three Ms:
    Market demand - is it something people want?
    Market structure - can you get an offering into the marketplace, and will people choose it over the other options?
    Market analysis - can you get it to the customer while still making a profit?

7 Ps of marketing:
    Product, price, place, promotiom, people, process, physical evidence

Brand - easily identified concept - easily remembered name, simple logo or brand message, identifiable + desirable attributes for the customer

Product differentiation explains what is special about the product - brands are worth more than product differentiation

Factors of brand personality - Sincerity, excitement, competence, sophistication, ruggedness

Promotion creates awareness of offering with an appropriate message to trigger interest

Segmentation - dividing a potential market up geographically, economically, demographically, lifestyle factors etc
    An example:
        The apple watch has titanium or silver/gold versions for high economy Customers
        It also has a sport version for sporty lifestyle people
        It also has a cheaper version with less features for lower economy customers
    
Targeting - focusing on a particular segment of the market - for example the Whoop band focues serious health fanatics in the fitness group, it doesnt even have a built in screen!

Positioning - strategy and product perception

Cost leadership competes on price, differentation competes on quality

Strategic capability - ability of a company to perform at the level required for success via their resources and competencies

Resources - resources that a company owns or can access to support its strategies

Competencies - what can your resources do and at what level?

Value chain analysis describes the seperate activities and analyses in terms of efficiency, effectiveness and coordination

Stars - high market share and high market growth
    products that perform strongly in fast growing markets
Cash Cows - High market share and low market growth
    Products that are well established in mature markets
Question marks - Low market share and high market growth
Dogs - low market share and low market growth
    weak market positions in slow growing markets

SWOT - strength weaknesses opportunities threats

Intellectual property - the various rights which protect you ideas or other forms of intellectual creation

Patent - duration limited territorial monopoly granted by government in return for public disclosure of new and
innovative products. They protect the application of such ideas
Gives you the right to prevent a 3rd party from using it, but not the right to us emit

Novelty - not found in a single document elsewhere
Innovative step  = idea shouldnt be apparent to an individual of ordinary ability practiced in the specific technology

Copyright - gives rights to the creator of a certain type of material
Not a registred form of protection, is automatic when work is made
Protects artistic work, literary work, drmama, audio or video
infringement is a criminal offence

Design - the external appearance of a product and features of its 3D shape and patterns/orientation in 2D
Registered design - New product designs having individual characters, must be new, never previously available and 
'individual in character'
Active for periods of 5 years, up to 25 years

A design right is a non registered form of protection against copying, weaker than design registration, it is not
a monopoly right, and has 10 year duration

A trade mark governs words, signs or graphics that distinguish a product or service, customers can identify the product
or service easily
Can last forever but must be renewed every 10 years

Know how is technical info, knowledge or skill, must be a secret, can be extremely valuable and can only be protected
by confidentiality agreements. For example - coca cola secret ingredient

Database rights protect collections of works or data, no ned to register and lasts 15 years

Geographical indications identify a good as originating in the territory of a member, or a region in that territory,
where has a given quality, reputation or other characteristics of the good is essentially attributable to its
graphical origin - e.g. Tequila or Champagne

Marketing research links an organisation to its market by gathering information that enables a company to identify 
market driven opportunities, refine and evaluate marketing actions and monitor overall marketing performance
    A form of social science
    Trying to make meaning by interpreting data from a number of sources

Knowlegde is at the top of the pyramid, and is based on interpretations (middle), the data is the bottom of the pyramid

Marketing research is used for future demand forecasting, analysing competitors, analysing opportunities for markets or companies
Also used for analysing potential of new market areas, identifying and evaluating markets for products and new products,
and sales forecasting

The customer challenge - customers dont often act as we expect

Type of research data:
    Secondary research - research that you have not carried out yourself, you just use the results (existing data)
    Primary research - data that you have gathered from your own research/testing
    Qualitative and quantitative

Primary data collection:
    Observation
    Surveys
    Content analysis

Business model - method for doing business by which a venture can generate revenue. Answers who the customers are and what
do they value?

Value proposition - the proposal of value to the customer by the company    
    Description of the bundle of the products and services that create value for the specific customers segments

Osterwalder's business model canvas:
    Key partners, key activities, value proposition, customer relationships, channels, customer segments,
    key resources, cost structure, revenue streams

Business model environment forces:
    Industry forces, macro economic forces, market foces and driving trends

Types of busines model:
    Resource driven - originate from existing infrastrcture/partnerships to expand or transform business model
    Offer driven - creates a new value propositions that affect other business model building blocks
    Customer driven - Based on customer needs, facilitated access, or increased convenience
    Finance driven - driven by new revenue streams, pricing mechanisms, or reduced cost structure that affect
    other business model building blocks
    Multiple epicentre driven - driven by multiple epicentres that can have significant impact on multiple other building blocks
